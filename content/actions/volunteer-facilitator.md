## What Volunteer Facilitators Do

You host a structured, 90-minute discussion about a policy topic in your community. We provide everything you need:

- A facilitation guide with prompts and timing
- A one-page briefing document for participants
- A digital or printable discussion kit
- A debrief call with our team after your event
- Access to a community of other facilitators

## What We're Looking For

We need facilitators who can:
- Listen more than they speak
- Hold space for disagreement without taking sides
- Follow a structured guide without improvising too much
- Report back honestly about what they heard

We do **not** need you to be a policy expert, a professional facilitator, or an activist. We actively want people from a wide range of backgrounds, beliefs, and communities.

## Where We Need Facilitators

We particularly need facilitators in:
- Rural and market-town communities in England, Wales, and Ireland
- Communities affected by industrial transition (former mining or manufacturing towns)
- Communities with high proportions of renters or housing-precarious residents
- Communities where distrust of public institutions is high

If you're not in these areas, we still want to hear from you — we'll find a use for a motivated facilitator.

## What You Get

- Training and ongoing support
- Printed and digital resources at no cost to you
- The satisfaction of running a genuinely useful civic conversation
- A certificate of participation
- An invitation to our annual facilitator gathering
