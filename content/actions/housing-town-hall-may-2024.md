## About This Event

This is a structured discussion — not a debate, not a protest, not a panel of talking heads. We bring together residents, local councillors, housing researchers, and planning professionals to work through the evidence on housing policy together.

The format:
1. **6:30pm** — Opening framing: what the evidence says about the housing shortfall
2. **7:00pm** — Policy options: zoning reform, social housing targets, rent stabilisation
3. **7:40pm** — Facilitated table discussions (8 people per table, structured prompts)
4. **8:10pm** — Group synthesis: what did we learn, what should we ask our councils?
5. **8:30pm** — Practical next steps: how to follow up with your local councillor
6. **9:00pm** — Close

## Who Should Attend

- Renters frustrated by the housing market and looking for practical next steps
- Homeowners concerned about neighbourhood change or planning decisions
- Local councillors and their advisors
- Housing charity staff, planning professionals, developers
- Anyone who wants to understand the policy options beyond the headlines

## What We Provide

All attendees receive:
- A one-page evidence brief on housing policy options
- A structured template for writing to your local council
- Links to the full policy analysis on this site
- A follow-up email with event notes and action items
