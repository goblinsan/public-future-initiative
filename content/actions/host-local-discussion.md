## Why Host a Discussion?

Policy is made by people who show up. That doesn't mean MPs or think-tank fellows — it means the residents who contact their councillor, the community members who write to a consultation, the citizens who know what's actually happening in their neighbourhood.

A structured discussion is not a protest and not a lecture. It's a room (or a Zoom) where people who live in the same place talk seriously about the same problems — guided by evidence, not ideology.

## What You'll Receive

When you register as a local organiser, we send you:
- A discussion guide with timing, prompts, and facilitation tips
- A one-page participant briefing on the topic you've chosen
- Printable materials (maps, charts, summary cards)
- A digital version for online sessions
- A debrief form to submit what you heard

Everything is free. We ask only for your honest report of what participants said.

## What Happens to Your Report

We aggregate reports from across the country to build a picture of what ordinary communities think about policy questions. This data is published on this site, shared with relevant parliamentary committees, and used to refine our own content.

Your participants are never identified. Location is reported only at city or county level unless you choose to share more.

## Choosing a Topic

We have discussion kits for:
- Guaranteed income and work
- Housing and planning
- AI, automation, and the future of work
- Climate policy and energy transition
- Healthcare access and NHS reform

If you want to run a discussion on a topic we don't have a kit for, [contact us](/contact) — we may be able to help.
