## What Is Universal Basic Income?

Universal Basic Income (UBI) is a policy proposal in which all citizens or residents of a country receive a regular, unconditional cash payment from the government, regardless of their employment status or income level.

## Core Principles

- **Universality** — paid to every eligible individual, not means-tested
- **Unconditionality** — no work requirements or behavioral conditions
- **Regularity** — paid at predictable intervals (monthly, weekly)
- **Cash** — recipients decide how to spend it

## Why It Matters

As automation reshapes the labor market and economic inequality grows, UBI is proposed as a tool to:
- Provide a floor of economic security for all citizens
- Reduce administrative complexity of means-tested welfare programs
- Enable people to take risks, retrain, and pursue unpaid caregiving

## Evidence and Debates

Evidence from pilots (including Stockton, CA and Finland) suggests UBI recipients show improved mental health, maintain or increase employment, and report greater agency. Critics raise concerns about inflation, work disincentives, and cost.
