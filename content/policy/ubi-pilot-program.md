## Policy Summary

This policy proposes federal funding for a structured, multi-city Universal Basic Income pilot program designed to generate rigorous evidence on UBI's effects in diverse American communities.

## Key Provisions

- **Duration**: 5 years
- **Payment**: $1,000/month per participant
- **Scale**: 30,000 participants across 3 cities
- **Design**: Randomized controlled trial with independent evaluation
- **Data**: Full public release of anonymized participant data post-study

## Expected Outcomes

- Peer-reviewed evidence on labor market effects
- Data on health, mental health, and well-being outcomes
- Cost-benefit analysis compared to existing programs
- Cross-city comparison enabling policy design recommendations

## Funding Mechanism

Proposed funding via a 0.1% financial transaction tax, estimated to generate $18B annually. Total program cost is estimated at $1.8B over five years, less than 0.1% of the federal discretionary budget.

## What the Evidence Says So Far

Smaller-scale pilots in Stockton (CA), Finland, and Kenya show consistent patterns: participants maintain or increase employment, report improved mental health, and use cash payments for basic needs rather than luxury goods. However, these pilots are not randomized at scale, making national policy extrapolation uncertain.

## Open Questions

- What payment level is sufficient to meaningfully reduce poverty without distorting labor markets?
- How should pilots account for macro-level effects that local experiments cannot capture?
- What institutional framework ensures rigorous independence of evaluation?
