## Overview

The Support Team Assisted Response (STAR) programme in Denver, Colorado, sends mental health clinicians and paramedics — instead of police officers — to respond to non-violent emergency calls involving mental health crises, welfare checks, and substance use. Launched as a pilot in June 2020 amid widespread debate about policing, STAR has since expanded significantly and become one of the most studied alternative emergency response models in the United States.

## What Was Tested

Denver asked: for the significant proportion of 911 calls that involve mental health, welfare concerns, or substance use with no violence or weapons, is a trained health-and-care response more effective than a police response?

STAR deployed two-person teams — a licensed mental health clinician paired with a paramedic — to respond to these call types across a defined area of Denver. Outcomes were tracked: did incidents escalate? Were arrests made? Were follow-up services accessed? Did costs differ?

## What Happened

In the first six months (a defined pilot area), STAR responded to over 700 calls. There were zero arrests and zero uses of force. Participants were connected to mental health, housing, and substance use services at significantly higher rates than historical averages for police responses to similar calls.

The programme handled 24% of all 911 calls in the pilot area, demonstrating the scale of the opportunity. Cost per intervention was substantially lower than police dispatch for equivalent call types.

By 2023, STAR had expanded to cover more of the city, fielded tens of thousands of calls, and was being studied by cities across the United States and internationally.

## Key Findings

- **Zero arrests and zero uses of force** across more than 700 calls in the initial pilot period
- STAR handled **24% of all 911 calls** in the pilot area — demonstrating the scale of non-police-appropriate calls
- Participants connected to follow-up mental health and social services at **higher rates** than comparison group
- **Cost per intervention lower** than police dispatch for equivalent call types
- The programme has since expanded and is being **replicated in cities across the US**
