## Overview

The Stockton Economic Empowerment Demonstration (SEED) was a 24-month guaranteed income pilot that ran from February 2019 through January 2021 in Stockton, California. 125 residents received $500/month with no strings attached, selected from neighbourhoods at or below the city's median household income ($46,033). A control group of 200 residents received no payment and was tracked for comparison.

## What Was Tested

SEED tested a simple hypothesis: would unconditional cash help low-income workers become more financially stable, healthier, and more able to pursue full-time employment — without reducing their motivation to work?

The programme made no requirements about how recipients spent the money, whether they worked, or what life choices they made.

## What Happened

Full-time employment among recipients increased from 28% to 40% over the two years (compared with 25% to 37% in the control group), suggesting the cash enabled rather than replaced work. Recipients reported significantly lower anxiety and depression at the 12- and 24-month marks.

Most spending went to food (35%), utilities (22%), and household goods — not alcohol, tobacco, or gambling. Average savings and credit scores among recipients improved modestly.

## Key Findings

- **Full-time employment** among recipients rose from 28% to 40% (vs. 25% to 37% in the control group)
- Recipients reported significantly **lower anxiety and depression**
- Most income was spent on **food, utilities, and household goods**
- No significant increase in spending on alcohol or tobacco
- Recipients showed improved **financial resilience** — less month-to-month income volatility
