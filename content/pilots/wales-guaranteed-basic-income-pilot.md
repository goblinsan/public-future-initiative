## Overview

In July 2022, the Welsh Government launched a Guaranteed Basic Income (GBI) pilot targeting care leavers — young people transitioning out of the care system at age 18. Around 500 young people across Wales receive £1,600 per month for two years with no conditions. The programme is one of the most generous basic income pilots anywhere in the world in terms of payment level.

Wales is using the pilot to test whether unconditional income can smooth the notoriously difficult transition out of care, a period when many young people face homelessness, unemployment, and mental health crises.

## What Was Tested

Care leavers in the UK face dramatically worse outcomes than the general population: higher rates of homelessness, unemployment, mental illness, and involvement with the criminal justice system. The Welsh Government asked: can a guaranteed income floor change these trajectories?

The pilot provides £1,600/month for two years to participants, starting from when they leave care at 18. No requirements are placed on how the money is spent or whether participants work, study, or train.

## What Happened

Interim results published in 2023 were cautiously positive. Participants reported significantly lower anxiety levels and greater confidence in their ability to manage their lives. Housing stability improved — fewer participants were experiencing temporary or insecure housing compared to care leavers in comparison groups.

Participants described using the income for rent deposits, driving lessons, training courses, and food — investments that previous care leavers often could not make. Several described being able to delay taking low-paid insecure work and instead pursue more stable longer-term options.

## Key Findings

- Interim findings show **significantly reduced anxiety and stress** among participants
- Improved **housing stability** compared to care leavers without the pilot income
- Participants report spending on **essentials and investment in futures** (training, transport, deposits)
- The pilot demonstrates **subnational basic income delivery** is logistically feasible
- Early data suggests recipients are **more likely to engage with education or training** than comparison groups
