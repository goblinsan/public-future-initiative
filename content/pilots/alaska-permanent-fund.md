## Overview

The Alaska Permanent Fund Dividend (PFD) is not an experiment — it is the world's longest-running universal cash transfer programme, now in its fifth decade. Every Alaskan resident who has lived in the state for at least one full year receives an annual dividend from a state-owned sovereign wealth fund built on oil revenues.

In 2023, the dividend was $1,312 per person. A family of four received $5,248. Since 1982, the fund has paid out more than $24 billion to Alaskans.

The Alaska Permanent Fund was created in 1976 by a constitutional amendment to save a share of the state's oil revenues for future generations. Since 1982, a portion of the fund's returns has been distributed annually to residents.

## What Was Tested

Alaska did not set out to run an experiment. But its 40+ year history provides unparalleled evidence on whether universal, unconditional cash payments to all residents affect work, poverty, and well-being.

## What Happened

Alaska has maintained low poverty rates and relatively high employment through the entire period. Academic research comparing Alaska to similar U.S. states before and after the dividend found no negative employment effect and a measurable reduction in poverty — particularly among Alaska Native communities.

A landmark 2018 study by Damon Jones and Ioana Marinescu using a synthetic control method found the dividend reduced the part-time employment rate but did not affect overall employment — suggesting recipients chose to work slightly fewer hours while still working.

## Key Findings

- Every Alaskan resident has received an **annual dividend since 1982** — the programme is now 40+ years old
- No evidence of reduced **employment or work motivation** at the population level
- Measurably **reduced poverty** — particularly among Alaska Native households
- Demonstrates that **public ownership of productive assets** can generate broadly shared returns
- The programme is **politically durable**: defended by Alaskans across the political spectrum
- Over $24 billion paid out to residents since 1982
