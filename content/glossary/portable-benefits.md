The concept of portable benefits addresses a structural mismatch in modern social insurance systems: most benefits were designed for a 20th-century economy in which workers spent careers with single employers. In a labor market with high job turnover, gig platforms, multiple part-time jobs, and self-employment, employer-tied benefits leave large gaps.

**What portability addresses:**

- Workers who change jobs lose health insurance during transitions
- Gig workers and contractors receive no employer contributions to retirement or paid leave
- Part-time workers often fall below hours thresholds that trigger benefit eligibility

**Implementation models include:**

- **Pro-rated employer contributions** — firms contribute to a worker's portable account in proportion to hours worked
- **Industry or sector funds** — especially in construction and care work, sector-wide benefit pools cover workers across multiple employers
- **State-level portable benefit systems** — proposed in several U.S. states

Portable benefits are often discussed as a complement to, rather than replacement for, more universal income floor proposals like guaranteed income or UBI.
