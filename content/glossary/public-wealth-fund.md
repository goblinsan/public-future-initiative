Public wealth funds represent a structural approach to broadening ownership of productive capital. Rather than relying solely on individual savings and private wealth accumulation, a public wealth fund allows the state to hold assets collectively — with returns benefiting all citizens rather than only those wealthy enough to own significant financial assets.

Existing examples include:

- **Norway's Government Pension Fund Global** — one of the world's largest sovereign wealth funds, built on oil revenues but invested in global equities and fixed income
- **Alaska Permanent Fund** — distributes annual dividends to Alaska residents from oil revenues (the basis for some UBI proposals)
- **Singapore's Temasek Holdings** — state-owned investment company holding major stakes in strategic sectors

Proposals for new public wealth funds often focus on using automation dividends or other technology revenues to build a fund that distributes returns to citizens, addressing the ownership gap created by AI-driven capital concentration.
