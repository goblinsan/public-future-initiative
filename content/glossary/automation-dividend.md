The automation dividend concept addresses the distributional problem at the heart of AI-era economics: when machines replace workers, productivity rises — but the gains flow primarily to the owners of those machines, not to displaced workers or the broader public.

Proposed mechanisms include:

- **Robot taxes** — levied on firms in proportion to the degree of automation in their workforce
- **Automation levies** — taxes on specific technology deployments
- **Data dividends** — compensation for the data provided by users and workers that trains AI systems
- **Revenue sharing mandates** — requirements that firms share productivity gains from automation with workers

No country has implemented a broad automation dividend at scale, but the concept has been debated seriously in policy circles in the United States, South Korea, and several European countries.
